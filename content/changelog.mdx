## v1.5.0 — July 12, 2026

A large content batch on the parts of the journey that had no page yet, plus the first section that names specific resources.

### Added

- **Small Things section** — a new top-level section on the habits, setup, and skills nobody puts on a syllabus, the kind of things that compound over a degree and get mistaken for talent later. Seven pages:
  - **Your Setup** — backups, file naming, password hygiene, exam-day network and power contingencies, and the free tools a student email unlocks (with a warning to claim them and move important accounts off the college email before it expires).
  - **Typing and Your Body** — touch typing, wrist and posture strain (roughly one in four keyboard workers hit repetitive strain injury), eyes, and screens, cross-linking Wellbeing for the placement-season depth.
  - **How to Learn** — reading the whole error message, searching the exact error string, debugging with a hypothesis, rubber duck and Feynman, recall over re-reading, and reading code you didn't write.
  - **Reaching Out** — email that gets read, cold messages that get replies, treating English as a practiced skill, and the follow-up and lurk-first habits.
  - **Keeping a Record** — a brag document, a one-line learned-log, READMEs, screen recordings, and small commits that write your resume for you.
  - **Reading the Room** — watching before you speak, disagreeing in private, mining seniors for specifics, and treating the people around you now as the network.
- **Learning Resources page** — the first and only place the guide names specific sources. A short, curated list of super-credible, mostly-free, unaffiliated resources, scoped to what outlasts any language: CS foundations, algorithms, system design, version control, and going deeper (OSSU, Teach Yourself CS, CS50, MIT Missing Semester, NeetCode, Tech Interview Handbook, Arpit Bhayani, System Design Primer, Pro Git, and more). It deliberately lists no language, framework, or web-stack tutorials, since the guide stays out of picking your stack. The Resources index and the home page both carry an explicit note that this section is the one exception to the no-recommendation rule.
- **Wellbeing became a section** — the single placement-season page grew into four. **Placement Season** keeps the physical grind (sleep, movement, food, anxiety). **Comparison and Imposter Syndrome** covers the highlight-reel feed and the fraud feeling. **Burnout** separates tired from burnt out and names the warning signs. **Family and Pressure** takes on the expectations, comparisons, and financial weight that nobody writes about.
- **Understanding Pay page** — where to find real salary numbers (levels.fyi, AmbitionBox, Glassdoor), how to convert a CTC into take-home, and how to read an offer past the headline. Cross-links the CTC breakdown on Placements & ROI.
- **Version Control page** (Learning to Program) — git and GitHub as guidance and pitfalls, not a tutorial: what a baseline looks like, never committing secrets, why GitHub is a public record, and pointers to the canonical free guides.
- **CGPA page** (The College Journey) — grades as a filter rather than a ranking, where they matter more (higher studies), the separate backlog problem, and the two opposite ways students get it wrong.
- **Glossary** — a new top-level reference page with plain-English, one-line definitions for the acronyms across the guide and placement season, grouped by college, pay, programming, tools, and higher studies.
- **Extended FAQs** — the College Journey and Resources index pages now answer the questions that come up most often, and Learning to Program gained questions on git and finding resources.

### Infrastructure

- **Sitemap** — added `sitemap.xml` generation via the Fumapress sitemap plugin, and a `robots.txt` pointing crawlers to it.
- Note for the record: full-text search (FlexSearch), `llms.txt` / `llms-full.txt`, and per-page OG images were already generated by existing Fumapress plugins, so those needed no new work.

### Changed

- **Home page and navigation** now feature the Small Things section (between Resources and Wellbeing) and the Glossary (before the Changelog). The Resources section gained the Learning Resources and Understanding Pay pages.

## v1.4.0 — July 10, 2026

A documentation-quality pass applying the principles from Fuma Nama's [How to Write Good Documentation](https://www.fuma-nama.dev/blog/good-docs) across the whole guide.

### Added

- **Page feedback widget** — every page now ends with a "Was this page helpful?" prompt. Yes/No responses are sent to PostHog, alongside one-click **Suggest an edit** and **Report a problem** links that open the source file or a pre-filled GitHub issue (these links work with no analytics at all). This closes the feedback loop the guide previously had no mechanism for.
- **FAQ sections** — the Choosing a College, Learning to Program, and Fourth Year pages now answer the questions that come up most often, each cross-linking to the page with the full answer.
- **Deep dive: CTC inflation** — the Placements & ROI page now explains *why* reported CTC runs 20–40% higher than take-home, with a table breaking down variable pay, bonuses, provident fund, and stock.

### Changed

- **Acronyms spelled out on first mention** — CSE, NAAC, NIRF, NBA, GSoC, CTC, LPA, ROI, IoT, CTF, DSA, OS, DBMS, OOP, HR, GATE, MS, MTech, SOP, GRE, and LOR are now expanded the first time they appear, so a reader landing on any page cold is not left guessing.
- **Links Worth Bookmarking** is now a table pairing each resource with what to use it for, instead of a run-on list.
- **Interview process** steps became headings with anchors rather than a numbered list, matching the guidance that meaningful headings beat sequential numbers.
- **Bare URLs became real hyperlinks** in the Resources section (Stack Overflow, the "Smart Questions" guide, the awesome-lists index).

### Fixed

- **Analytics were silently dead.** The PostHog client read `VITE_`-prefixed environment variables, but Waku only exposes `WAKU_PUBLIC_`-prefixed vars to the browser — so page views (and the new feedback events) were never sent, in dev or production. Switched to `WAKU_PUBLIC_POSTHOG_KEY` / `WAKU_PUBLIC_POSTHOG_HOST`, added a `.env.example`, and made the provider skip initialization when unconfigured instead of calling `init(undefined)` and dropping every event.
- The feedback widget now resets per page (keyed by route) instead of carrying its answered state across client-side navigation.
- Replaced mangled em-dashes that rendered as `" , "` across the Choosing a College section (accreditation, placements, sub-branches, admissions, links, and culture pages).
- Configured the repository so **Edit this page** and view-source links resolve correctly instead of silently doing nothing.

### Added

Five sections launched with full content:

- **Choosing a College** — 10 pages covering how to build a shortlist, evaluate intake size, assess coding culture, understand internship policies, read sub-branch naming (AI/Data Science/Cyber Security), interpret placement data honestly, navigate admission paths, and factor in location.
