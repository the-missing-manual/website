Use Google, ask people, ask an AI for names. Build a rough list first, then filter it.

| Tier | Criteria |
|------|----------|
| **Tier 1** | NIRF engineering rank under 100, or a central/deemed university with national recognition |
| **Tier 2** | NIRF rank 100 to 300, or a college with strong state-level reputation |
| **Tier 3** | Everything else |

Once you have names, run them through the filters below.


## Accreditation , Both Kinds

Most students check NAAC and stop there. That is not enough.

**NAAC grade** tells you about the institution overall. Aim for **A or above**. The grade expires every five years. <Mark>Check when it was last issued.</Mark> A college with an A grade from six years ago is running on outdated credentials and may not have renewed.

**NBA accreditation** is different. It is branch-specific, not college-wide. A college can hold an A-grade NAAC and still have a CSE program with no NBA accreditation. NBA means that specific branch has been evaluated against quality benchmarks. If it does not have it, ask why.

<Callout type="info">
Check both. NAAC for the institution, NBA specifically for the CSE branch you are applying to.
</Callout>


## Autonomous vs. Affiliated

Check whether the college is **autonomous**.

Affiliated colleges follow their parent university's regulations: the syllabus, the exams, the electives. An autonomous college sets its own. For CSE this matters because software changes fast. An autonomous college can update its syllabus, add modern electives, and rework assessments. <Mark>An affiliated college often cannot, at least not quickly.</Mark>

This distinction is worth checking closely in Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu, where the gap between the two is wide.
