Every roadmap covers the skills and skips the person learning them. Then the degree turns out to run on a body that needs sleep and a mind that compares itself to everyone else, and none of that was in the plan.

This section is about that part. It's not motivational, and it's not a substitute for real help when you need it. It's the honest physical and mental reality of the years you're in, and the things that keep you functional through them.

<Mark>You can prepare perfectly and still fall apart from bad sleep, constant comparison, or pressure nobody helped you carry. The person doing the work needs maintenance too.</Mark>

### Common Questions

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Is losing sleep to study before an interview worth it?">
    Almost never. Working memory degrades with sleep loss, so a rested twenty-minute problem becomes a tired forty-five-minute one. Set a hard stop and review what you know. See [Placement Season](/wellbeing/placement-season).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Everyone on LinkedIn seems ahead of me. How do I deal with it?">
    You're seeing offers, not the rejections behind them. The only honest comparison is you against yourself last month. See [Comparison and Imposter Syndrome](/wellbeing/comparison).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I'm exhausted and rest isn't fixing it. Is that normal?">
    That's the line between tired and burnt out. Tiredness lifts with a weekend. Burnout needs a change of pace, not just a nap. See [Burnout](/wellbeing/burnout).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My family's expectations are a lot. Is that a wellbeing issue?">
    Yes, and a common one nobody writes about. Naming the weight is the first step to carrying it on your own terms. See [Family and Pressure](/wellbeing/pressure).
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
