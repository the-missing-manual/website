Most of this section teaches a skill rather than handing you a link: how to find what you need yourself, judge whether it's worth your time, and use it without getting stuck in consumption loops. That skill outlasts any list.

<Mark>The ability to find, evaluate, and use information is more durable than any specific list of resources.</Mark>

<Callout type="info" title="The one exception to the no-recommendation rule">
The rest of this guide names no specific tools, courses, or channels, on purpose. Resources is the single place it does. [Learning Resources](/resources/learning) and [Understanding Pay](/resources/compensation) point to a short list of super-credible, mostly-free sources with no affiliation to this guide. Nobody paid to be listed. Everything else on this site stays neutral.
</Callout>

### Common Questions

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Why does the rest of the guide avoid recommending resources, but this section doesn't?">
    Because ranking tools and courses is where guides get captured by sponsorships and go stale. The [Learning Resources](/resources/learning) list sidesteps both: everything on it is widely respected, mostly free, and unaffiliated. It stays short for the same reason.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="There are ten resources for every topic. How do I pick one?">
    Pick the one you'll actually finish, then stop shopping. Switching resources every week is a way to feel busy without learning. Cross-check a couple, commit to one, and build alongside it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I keep watching tutorials but not improving. What's wrong?">
    You're consuming, not doing. A tutorial you watch without writing code is entertainment. Build the thing as you go, and see [Searching Effectively](/resources/google-search) for finding the specific answer instead of another full course.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I figure out real salary numbers for a company?">
    Cross-check self-reported data across a few sources and convert CTC to in-hand before comparing. See [Understanding Pay](/resources/compensation).
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
