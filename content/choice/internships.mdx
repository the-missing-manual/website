---
title: Internship Policies
description: Questions to ask before you join, not after.
---


Most good software jobs come through **internship conversions**, not direct campus placements. A college that blocks you from taking internships during the academic year is taking something real away from you, and it will not say that in the brochure.


### The Attendance Problem

Check the **attendance policy** before anything else.

Some colleges enforce 85% attendance and bar you from sitting exams if you fall below it, regardless of how you perform otherwise. <Mark>This directly affects your ability to attend internships, hackathons, and external events during the semester.</Mark>

<Callout type="warn">
Ask students about attendance enforcement. Not the admissions office. The admissions office will tell you it is flexible. Students will tell you whether it actually is.
</Callout>


### Questions to Ask Before Joining

Get answers to these from current students, not college representatives:

- Do students take semester-long internships?
- Are remote internships counted toward attendance?
- Do final-year students do six-month internships?
- Does the college actively support internship participation, or does it create friction?
- Has anyone been penalized for attending an internship or external event?


### How to Find Students

LinkedIn is the most reliable way. Search the college name, filter by current students, and message a few. Keep it short: one or two specific questions, not a paragraph asking them to evaluate their entire college experience.

Also search:

```
[College Name] software engineer
[College Name] SDE
[College Name] backend engineer
[College Name] Google
[College Name] Microsoft
```

You want evidence that graduates consistently enter the software industry, not only IT service roles. If the alumni results are mostly from service companies, that tells you where the college's placement relationships are concentrated.

When you talk to students, also ask:

- How often do students attend external hackathons?
- Is there an approval process for attending events?
- Does the college sponsor travel or registrations for competitions?
- Are there industry talks or meetups students actually attend?

After three or four conversations, patterns become clear quickly.
