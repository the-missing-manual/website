### Placement Quality, Not Quantity

For CSE, separate placements into categories before you read any number:

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Service companies | TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant |
| Product companies | Microsoft, Google, Adobe, Atlassian, Salesforce |
| Startups | varies |
| Research labs | varies |

A college reporting 95% placement might be placing most students into service roles at 3.5 to 5 LPA. That is a fundamentally different outcome from a college that consistently sends students to product companies. The headline number does not distinguish between the two.

Ask which **product companies visit campus consistently**, not which ones visited once three years ago.

Do not fixate on the highest CTC. Look at the **average package**, what percentage of students are placed, and the **median** if it is published. <Mark>Half the placed students earned the median or below. That number is almost always more honest than the average.</Mark>

<Callout type="warn">
Missing data is data. If a college publishes the highest package but not the median, the placement percentage, or a full placement report, ask why. Colleges that are proud of their placements publish everything.
</Callout>


### Cross-Check the Numbers

College-reported CTC figures are frequently **20 to 40 percent higher** than what those companies actually pay freshers. Cross-reference company names from placement reports on [AmbitionBox](https://www.ambitionbox.com) or Glassdoor before treating any figure as accurate.


### Calculating ROI

To check whether the fees make sense relative to the outcome:

$$
\text{ROI Ratio} = \frac{\text{Average CTC} \times 3}{\text{Total 4-year Fees}}
$$

- **Average CTC** , what most placed students earn, not the highest
- **x 3** , rough estimate of your first three years of income
- **Total 4-year Fees** , everything you pay the college, excluding hostel and living costs

| Ratio | What it means |
|-------|---------------|
| Above 2.0 | Fees recover in a reasonable time |
| 1.0 to 2.0 | Worth scrutinizing further |
| Below 1.0 | You are paying more than three years of salary to get the degree |

Also factor in the total cost beyond tuition. Hostel, mess, travel, and fee hikes across four years add up significantly. <Mark>Never treat an education loan as future you's problem.</Mark>
