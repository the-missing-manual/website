---
title: Sub-Branches
description: CSE AI, Data Science, Cyber Security — when they make sense and when they do not.
---

CSE AI, CSE Data Science, CSE Cyber Security, CSE IoT (Internet of Things). These exist in large numbers now. <Mark>Most were created for intake expansion, not academic depth.</Mark> At the bachelor's level, the subject difference between core CSE and most of these streams is small. You will study theory either way.

That said, there are situations where a sub-branch is worth taking seriously.


### When a Sub-Branch Actually Makes Sense

**The subjects are genuinely different.** Not renamed, not reordered. Actually different. A Cyber Security program with dedicated labs and an active Capture The Flag (CTF) team is meaningfully different from one that adds a security elective to a standard CSE curriculum. A Data Science program where faculty publish research and students work on real datasets is different from one that rebranded its statistics course.

**It has its own placement track.** Some sub-branches, particularly Cyber Security and Data Science at a small number of colleges, have relationships with companies that recruit specifically for those roles. If the placement outcomes in that branch are different from core CSE, that is worth investigating.

**It is your only option at an otherwise strong college.** In that case, the branch name matters far less than the environment around you.


### The Question That Actually Matters

Ask students in that specific branch: what did you study, what did you build, and where did you get placed?

The answer tells you whether the sub-branch has real depth or is a renamed program.

<Callout type="warn" title="Watch for this pattern">
A college markets AI, ML, Data Science, or Cyber Security heavily in its brochure but has no active student projects, no research groups, no hackathon wins in those areas, and no placement outcomes in those domains.

**"We have an AI lab"** is not evidence. Student projects on GitHub are. GSoC selections are. Hackathon wins in ML tracks are.

A large number of colleges renamed programs faster than they built any actual expertise in them.
</Callout>
