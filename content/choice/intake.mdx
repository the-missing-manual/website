---
title: Intake Size
description: Why the number of seats matters more than any placement headline.
---

Before spending time on clubs, placements, or internship policies, check this first.

A college that originally had **180 CSE seats** might now have **1500+ students** across CSE and its sub-branches. That affects placement competition, faculty attention, lab resources, and project opportunities. <Mark>The placement percentages often do not reflect this because the denominator quietly grew.</Mark>

A college reporting 90% placement across 180 students is a very different situation from 90% across 1500.


### What to Check

Check the **total intake across all CSE-related branches**, not only the one you are applying to. Add up CSE, CSE AI, CSE Data Science, CSE Cyber Security, and any other variant the college runs.

Then ask students, not the admissions office, how many people competed for the same companies during placements. That number tells you more than the headline figure.

<Callout type="warn">
Colleges do not always advertise intake expansion. If the NIRF report shows a jump in enrolled students over three to four years, investigate that before assuming the placement statistics still mean what they did.
</Callout>
